Platform overview

A platform for simulation practice, reasoning, and formative feedback.

Empathetics is built for education providers who want more than a fixed library or a public-facing student product. It supports educator-shaped experiences that can be piloted, evaluated, and expanded in ways that make sense for real programs.

Simulation practice

Support learners with scenario-based experiences that rehearse communication, judgement, and applied decision-making in safer, lower-stakes contexts.

Curriculum-aligned feedback

Keep educator intent close to the learner experience by shaping prompts, criteria, and feedback around your teaching objectives and standards.

Institutional pilots

Start with a defined use case, a real cohort, and a practical delivery model, then decide how far to scale based on evidence from your own context.

What the platform can support

A broader set of learning experiences than one product label suggests.

Health is the lead domain, but Empathetics is not limited to a single use case. The platform can support multiple patterns of practice and reflection across professional education.

Scenario-driven simulations

Build interactions around difficult conversations, reasoning checkpoints, follow-up reviews, or profession-specific performance moments.

Practice with structured feedback

Use the platform to reinforce habits of reflection, communication quality, and applied reasoning rather than only content recall.

Program-specific pilot pathways

Frame the first deployment around the program, the cohort, and the implementation appetite you actually have, not an imagined one.

Examples

Example pilot directions

Clinical communication

Rehearse history-taking, handover, patient explanation, or difficult discussion skills with opportunities for repetition and reflection.

Reasoning-heavy tasks

Support learners to explain decisions, justify priorities, and work through problem-solving steps in ways that make thinking more visible.

Professional practice beyond health

Extend the same rehearsal logic to law and other professional programs where judgement, dialogue, and feedback matter.
