Extending Meaningful Learning Beyond the Classroom

Empathetics increases the amount of meaningful, interactive learning and feedback each student receives — not by replacing educators, but by extending educator-designed interactions beyond the classroom at a fraction of the cost of equivalent educator-mediated time.

Why Interaction Matters for Learning

Effective learning does not occur through exposure alone. Research grounded in the constructivist tradition consistently shows that learning is strongest when students actively construct understanding through authentic tasks, interaction, and feedback.

Authenticity

Learning activities are most effective when they reflect real-world application. Complex professional tasks can be broken into smaller, meaningful components (part-practice), allowing students to practise realistic elements of clinical or professional work before integrating them into full interactions.

Interactivity

Learning requires action. By asking students to speak, write, and respond, interactive tasks require learners to articulate reasoning, test ideas, and make decisions. Part-practice interactions allow students to focus on specific skills in a low-risk environment before progressing to more complex scenarios.

Individualised Feedback

Feedback is essential for learning, but providing timely, individualised feedback at scale is challenging. Authentic, interactive learning experiences often require experienced educators or trained actors — resources that are effective, but costly and difficult to scale consistently.

Empathetics is designed to support these evidence-based learning mechanisms, increasing not just the amount, but the quality of student interaction time, without proportionally increasing educator workload.

How Empathetics Supports Learning

Empathetics translates learning science into practical teaching tools that remain firmly under educator control.

Authentic Practice

Empathetics allows educators to create interactions that closely mirror real-world clinical and professional encounters. Fully customisable voice-to-voice conversations with AI actors enable students to practise realistic scenarios in a safe, supported environment.

Active Interaction

Students learn by participating. Empathetics requires learners to actively engage through speaking, writing, and decision-making, supporting experimentation, reflection, and confidence-building through repeated, structured practice.

Personalised Feedback at Scale

Educators can embed tailored feedback directly into simulations, guiding students toward learning objectives during and after interactions. This delivers personalised, curriculum-aligned formative feedback without increasing marking or supervision demands.

Interaction Capacity in Context

Understanding the limits of educator-supervised interaction time — and where Empathetics fits.

Traditional Supervised Teaching

  • 120 students delivered in cohorts of 40
  • 2-hour workshops with 3 educators
  • 10 workshops per semester, per course

Educator Interaction Capacity

  • 6 educator-hours per workshop (~360 minutes)
  • ≈ 9 minutes of educator-supervised interaction per student
  • Increasing this time requires proportional increases in staffing

Empathetics as an Adjunct

  • ~10 minutes of structured, interactive learning per student
  • Embedded feedback aligned to curriculum goals
  • Designed to complement educator-led teaching

Empathetics delivers low-cost, semi-supervised interaction that increases total meaningful engagement per student — without increasing educator workload.
